Autonomous Dish Loading via Perception-to-Grasp Pipeline
Bido Mohamed (Supervised by Andrew Goldenberg)
Undergraduate Thesis — Robotics & Automation Lab, University of Toronto, 2025–2026
Developed a modular YOLOv8/GR-ConvNet perception-to-grasp pipeline for autonomous dish loading in cluttered PyBullet environments. Optimized pick sequences via Held-Karp TSP, achieving 0.995 mAP50 and 83% grasp success across 2,000 validation scenes.

Electrochemical Additive Optimization for Zinc Battery Cycle Life
Bido Mohamed (Supervised by Rutendo Mutambanengwe)
R&D Internship — e-Zinc, Mississauga, ON — Aug 2024 – Mar 2025
Conducted electrochemical experiments to evaluate 12 additive formulations, optimizing battery cycle life and improving charging efficiency by 7%.
